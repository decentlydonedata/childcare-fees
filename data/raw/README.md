December Quarter 2018 to December Quarter 2024:
Centre Based Day Care Average fees per hour by SA3
    * Centre Based Day Care (CBDC) typically provides care in centres with qualified educators. It is sometimes known as ‘long day care'. Services are typically open Monday to Friday and cater for children from birth to school age. Children are usually grouped with others of their own age
    * Average fees per hour refers to the fees before subsidise are added
    * Statistical Area 3 refers to the geographical unit within the Australian Statistical Geography Standard (ASGS) used by the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) to release regional data. They are designed to represent functional areas with populations between 30,000 and 130,000 people, often aligning with regional cities or major transport/commercial hubs.
VARIABLES
    "SA4 Code" = Statistical Area 4 code defined by the ASGS (string)
    "SA4 Name" = Name of Statistical Area 4 code defined by the ASGS (string)
    "State" = Australian State/Territory (string)
    "SA3 Code" = Statistical Area 3 code defined by the ASGS (string)
    "SA3 Name" = Name of Statistical Area 3 code defined by the ASGS (string)
    "service count" = Refers to the count of approved childcare services overseen by The Australian Children’s Education & Care Quality Authority (ACECQA) (int)
    "Mean fee per hour" = Average fee per child per hour in nominal Australian Dollars (double)
    "% Growth in Mean fee" = Percentage change in nominal fees from previous year's quarter (double)
    "Number of services above cap" = Number of services charging fees higher that the Child Care Subsidy (CCS) fee cap
    "% services above the cap" = Proportion of total services charging fees higher than the CCS fee cap
    
Limitations:
    * ASGS editions have changed between 2016 and 2021, therefore analysis made between certain SA3 may have some loss of accuracy
    * Period of June 2020 is missing

December Quarter 2018 to December Quarter 2024:
Index numbers; Childcare
    * The price of child care in the CPI is equal to the gross fee payable by the parents, less the amount of subsidies directly related to child care services that they receive. It is measured on an out-of-pocket expenses basis as follows: Net Child Care Fees = Gross Child Care Fees - Child Care Benefit - Child Care Rebate.
VARIABLES
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Sydney" = Net Childcare Fees in Sydney
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Melbourne" = Net Childcare Fees in Melbourne
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Brisbane" = Net Childcare Fees in Brisbane
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Adelaide" = Net Childcare Fees in Adelaide
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Perth" = Net Childcare Fees in Perth
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Hobart" = Net Childcare Fees in Hobart
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Darwin" = Net Childcare Fees in Darwin
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Canberra" = Net Childcare Fees in Canberra
    "Index numbers; Childcare; Australia" = Weighted average of Net Childcare Fees of capital cities
    "Index numbers; All groups; Sydney" = Weighted average of prices in Sydney
    "Index numbers; All groups; Melbourne" = Weighted average of prices in Melbourne
    "Index numbers; All groups; Brisbane" = Weighted average of prices in Brisbane
    "Index numbers; All groups; Adelaide" = Weighted average of prices in Adelaide
    "Index numbers; All groups; Perth" = Weighted average of prices in Perth
    "Index numbers; All groups; Hobart" = Weighted average of prices in Hobart
    "Index numbers; All groups; Darwin" = Weighted average of prices in Darwin
    "Index numbers; All groups; Canberra" = Weighted average of prices in Canberra
    "Index numbers; All groups; Australia" = Weighted average of prices of capital cities
Limitations:
    * Since the Consumer Price index (CPI) is measured via a weighted average of the capital cities as defined by their Greater Capital City Statistical Area, the CPI of non-capital city localities can only be assumed, estimated or excluded from analysis.
